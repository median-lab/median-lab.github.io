The University and the City

Established in 2012, the Southern University of Science and Technology (SUSTech, https://www.sustech.edu.cn/en/) is a public institution funded by the municipal of Shenzhen, a special economic zone city in China. Shenzhen is a major city located in Southern China, situated immediately north to Hong Kong Special Administrative Region. As one of China's major gateways to the world, Shenzhen is the country's fastest-growing city in the past two decades. The city is the high-tech and manufacturing hub of southern China, home to the world's third-busiest container port, and the fourth-busiest airport on the Chinese mainland. It is widely regarded as China's silicon valley with a strong IT industry, including Tencent, Huawei, etc. As a picturesque coastal city, Shenzhen is also a popular tourist destination and was named one of the world's 31 must-see tourist destinations in 2010 by The New York Times. At the end of 2018, there were around 20 million residents in Shenzhen. SUSTech is a pioneer in higher education reform in China. The mission of the University is to become a globally recognized research university which emphasizes academic excellence and promotes innovation, creativity and entrepreneurship. It is ranked the 8th in mainland China by THES and the 14th by QS in 2020. Set on five hundred acres of wooded landscape in the picturesque Nanshan (South Mountain) area, the campus offers an ideal environment for learning and research.

The Department

The Department of Computer Science and Engineering (CSE, http://cse.sustc.edu.cn/en/) at the Southern University of Science and Technology (SUSTech) was founded in 2016. As a young and ambitious CSE department in the heart of China's silicon valley --- Shenzhen, it has grown to 42 full-time professors in 2020, including tenured, tenure-track, research-only and teaching-only professors. Among them are three IEEE fellows and two (former) editors-in-chief of IEEE Transactions/journals and winners of prestigious international awards. Over 90% professors were recruited directly from outside mainland China. In the last four years, the Department has secured ~200 millions RMB (~28 millions USD) external grants. The Department plans to double its size in the next five years. The language of teaching at SUSTech is English.
